HOW IT WORKS — with the sources open

The whole machine is documented, and every claim links into the real assembly at the v1.0 tag: ARCHITECTURE — the 64K one-segment discipline, the memory map, where it stops and why · 3D GRAPHICS — painter's algorithm three ways, fixed-point trig, the guarded projection, the hardened clipper · EXAMPLES — standalone teaching .COMs a few KB each: the island factory, the ring mixer, the hangar.

THE WAR

Two squadrons in an attrition war over a procedural island: VIPERS — you plus 19 green squares — against 20 BANDIT yellow diamonds. The dead stay dead; when a team is wiped out the winner banner lights up and two fresh squadrons take the sky. The radar scoreboard shows this round's kills per team (tagged with the team's marker shape); the white number is your personal log and survives across rounds.

A jet soaks 2–5 cannon hits or one missile — and so do the buildings: a levelled block breaks into wall slabs, burns where it falls and stays a ruin forever. Falling wreckage can bring the neighbours down in a chain. Everything collides: jets with jets, jets with towers, wrecks with everything. Ramming works. Once.

You take 5 hits — the aiming ring flashes red and pips light up on the radar. The fifth, or a hillside, replays your last five seconds as a flashback, then the camera watches your jet come apart. GAME OVER — Space — again.

Watch the tapes: the red line on the speed tape is the stall, the one on the altitude tape is the terrain right below you. The gunsight bar always stays level with the horizon and its tick points at the sky; lose the horizon in a loop and a standby horizon appears at the ring.

THE PANEL

Left MFD — radar (heading-up): you are the centre tick, blips are jets (IFF-coloured, a dot above/below = higher/lower than you), amber sparks are missiles in the air, pips are your rails, the amber number is the cannon belt, red pips are your damage. Centre MFD — artificial horizon. Right MFD — the island map, or the target camera (M). Caution letters on the struts: Fuel, Stall, Terrain, Lock.
